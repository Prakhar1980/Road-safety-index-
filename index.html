Who are we?

More than 1 million people die in road traffic accidents each year. Road Safety Index tells you the roadâ€™s vulnerability score to accidents, helping you choose your way safely.

The Road Safety Index (RSI) is a program based on a machine learning algorithm that predicts how vulnerable a road is to accidents. It uses a training dataset consisting of 500 images from India (regularly updated) and their vulnerability scores obtained from surveys. It differentiates images based on features such as textures, colors, and shapes, assigning a score based on learned associations.

Connect With Us

This project is useful for social researchers and can be a valuable source of data for comparison with real-world data. Scientists can analyze accidents and the scores generated by this algorithm.

Feel free to upload images of roads outside India; it will help us improve the algorithm.

Road Safety Index Range Criteria

Classification of Streets with their RSIs

High RSI
Highways, Properly maintained Roads
RSI: 8-10

Moderate RSI
Empty roads with fewer people and vehicles
RSI: 4-6

Low RSI
Narrow streets with crowd, pits, etc.
RSI: 1-3
